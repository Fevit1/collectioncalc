About Slab Worthy™

Slab Worthy™ is a pre-assessment tool that helps comic collectors decide whether professional grading makes financial sense. We analyze photos of your comic to estimate its condition and compare the cost of grading against the likely value increase.

Think of us as a knowledgeable friend helping you make informed decisions before spending money on professional grading services like CGC or CBCS.

Upload 4 photos of your comic (front cover, spine, back cover, and centerfold). Our AI analyzes these images to:

  • Identify the comic title and issue
  • Detect visible defects (creases, tears, spine stress, etc.)
  • Estimate the likely grade range
  • Calculate current market values (raw and slabbed)
  • Recommend whether professional grading is worth the cost

You'll get a detailed Slab Report with our assessment and recommendation.

Pricing details are available on our website. We offer different tiers depending on how many comics you want to assess.

Most assessments complete in 1-2 minutes after you upload all 4 photos, though it can be faster if we've previously assessed this comic in similar condition. The process involves identifying your comic, analyzing photos for defects, and querying market data to calculate values.

Taking Photos

You need 4 photos:

  • Front Cover: Full front of the comic (required)
  • Spine: The comic's spine/edge
  • Back Cover: Full back of the comic
  • Centerfold: Open the comic to the middle pages

Click "Photo tips" during upload for detailed guidance on lighting, angle, and focus.

While all 4 photos provide the most accurate assessment, you can proceed with fewer photos. The front cover is required at minimum.

Note that assessments with fewer photos will be less accurate, particularly for detecting spine stress, interior defects, and centerfold issues. We'll note this limitation in your Slab Report.

Good photos are:

  • Well-lit with natural, indirect light (avoid glare and shadows)
  • In focus with sharp, readable text
  • Taken straight-on (camera parallel to comic)
  • Framed with the comic filling most of the image
  • Shot on a plain, neutral background (white or black preferred)

Blurry, dark, or angled photos may reduce assessment accuracy.

Currently, you assess one comic at a time. After completing an assessment, click "Check Another" to start a new one. All your assessments are saved in "My Collection" for easy reference.

We're considering batch upload and video upload features for future updates to make the process even faster.

Assessment & Valuation

Our assessments are designed to give you informed guidance, not guarantees. Accuracy depends on several factors:

  • Photo quality: Clear, well-lit photos produce better results
  • Visible defects: We can only detect what's visible in photos (hidden defects like interior foxing may not be caught)
  • Market data: Values are based on recent sales, which can fluctuate

Use our assessments as a helpful guide for decision-making, not as a replacement for professional grading. Think of it like getting advice from a knowledgeable collector friend—valuable insight, but the final decision is yours.

Our algorithm analyzes many factors to estimate what your comic would realistically sell for, including:

  • Recent sales data from tens of thousands of sales and growing every day
  • Comparable condition and grade ranges
  • Edition variations and variants
  • Current market demand and trends
  • Time-based pricing patterns

Market values can fluctuate based on demand, recent movie announcements, comic events, and other factors.

Raw value is what your comic is worth as-is, ungraded and unencapsulated.

Slabbed value is the estimated worth if professionally graded and encapsulated by CGC or CBCS. Slabbing typically increases value because:

  • The grade is verified by a trusted third party
  • The comic is protected from further damage
  • It's more marketable to serious collectors

However, slabbing costs money. Our recommendation factors in whether the value increase justifies the grading cost.

We recommend keeping a comic raw when the cost of professional grading would exceed the likely value increase. This happens when:

  • The comic has significant defects that would result in a low grade
  • The market value difference between raw and slabbed is small
  • Grading fees (typically $20-100+) wouldn't be recovered in resale value

Of course, if you want to slab it for personal enjoyment or long-term collection value, that's a valid choice too. Our recommendation is purely financial.

Slab Guard™

Slab Guard is our comic authentication and theft protection service. When you register a comic, our AI generates a unique visual fingerprint using Amazon Rekognition and assigns a serial number that can be publicly verified by anyone.

Every physical comic is unique — microscopic differences in printing, wear patterns, and paper texture make each copy one-of-a-kind. Our AI analyzes your comic's photos to create a visual fingerprint tied to that specific physical copy, not just the title or issue number.

After grading a comic, save it to your collection. You'll see a "Register This Comic" button on the grade report. Click it and your comic will be assigned a unique serial number (format: SW-YYYY-NNNNNN). Registration is available on the Guard plan and above.

Anyone can verify a comic at slabworthy.com/verify — no login required. Enter the serial number and you'll see the comic's details, registration date, and current status. If the status shows "Reported Stolen," do not purchase and report to authorities.

You can also upload a photo to check it against the entire registry — no serial number needed.

The free plan includes 3 comic registrations so you can try it out. The Guard plan includes 25 registrations, and Dealer plans include unlimited registrations. See our pricing page for details.

Slab Guard includes a free Chrome extension that monitors eBay listings in real time. When you browse eBay comic search results, the extension automatically checks listing images against the Slab Guard registry and overlays status badges directly on the page — red for reported stolen, green for registered and verified.

You can also manually check any individual eBay listing with one click. The extension is available in the Chrome Web Store and works with any Slab Worthy account.

When registering a comic with Slab Guard, photo quality is your proof of ownership. High-resolution photos taken directly with your phone's camera contain rich detail that simply can't be replicated from a screenshot or web-saved image. A 12-megapixel photo captures ink lines, paper texture, and unique wear patterns with a level of fidelity that a 0.3-megapixel eBay screenshot never could.

Think of it this way: if you have the comic in your hands, you can always take a high-quality photo. If you don't, you can't. That's what makes original, full-resolution photos the strongest ownership evidence in the registry.

For the best registration:

  • Use your phone's camera at full resolution (don't reduce image quality in settings)
  • Upload directly from your camera roll or Google Photos/iCloud — not from messaging apps
  • Don't crop, filter, or edit photos before uploading
  • Good lighting helps — but resolution matters more than perfect lighting for registration

When you register a comic, our system performs multi-signal forensic analysis on your uploaded photos to verify they were taken directly with your camera. We analyze metadata, image quality, compression patterns, lighting characteristics, and other indicators to detect screenshots, web-saved images, or photos of screens.

This protects the registry from bad actors who might try to claim ownership of someone else's comic using photos grabbed from online listings.

Tip: For the best results, upload photos directly from your phone's camera roll or cloud photos (Google Photos, iCloud). Photos sent through messaging apps like WhatsApp lose quality and metadata, which may trigger additional verification steps.

If our system flags your photo as potentially not being an original camera photo, don't worry — it doesn't mean you did anything wrong. Photos sent through messaging apps, downloaded from cloud services, or heavily compressed may trigger a review.

If flagged, you may be asked to provide additional proof of possession, such as taking a new photo of the comic with a specific item next to it. This extra step helps keep the registry trustworthy for everyone.
