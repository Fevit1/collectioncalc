FAQ

Everything you need to know about Slab Worthy.

What is Slab Worthy? +
Slab Worthy is an AI-powered tool that helps comic collectors decide whether a comic is worth professional grading ("slabbing"). You snap 4 photos, our AI estimates the grade, and we calculate whether the expected value increase justifies the grading cost.
How accurate is the grade estimate? +
Our AI provides an estimate based on visible defects, not a guarantee. We show a confidence percentage with every grade. For most comics, we're within ±0.5 grade points of actual CGC results. That said, professional graders have the final say—we help you decide which comics are worth submitting.
Why do you need 4 photos? +
Different defects appear in different places. The front cover shows color breaks and corner wear. The spine reveals ticks and stress marks. The back shows additional wear. And the centerfold can expose hidden issues like rust migration, staple damage, or detached pages. Four photos = more accurate grades.
What does "Worth the Slab" mean? +
It means we think you'll make money by grading. We compare the raw (ungraded) value to the expected slabbed value at your estimated grade, then subtract grading fees, shipping, and handling time. If the math works out positive, it's "Worth the Slab." If not, we'll tell you to "Keep it Raw."
Where does the pricing data come from? +
We pull actual sold listings from eBay and Whatnot—real transactions, not asking prices. This gives you market-based valuations, not guesses. We filter out reprints, variants, and outliers to give you the most accurate comps.
What comics can I grade? +
Currently we support US comics from major publishers (Marvel, DC, Image, Dark Horse, etc.) from the Golden Age through Modern Age. We're expanding coverage regularly. If we can't identify your comic, we'll let you know.
Is my data safe? +
Yes. We don't sell your data or share your collection details. Photos are processed for grading and stored securely. You can delete your account and data at any time. See our Privacy Policy for full details.
What's the patent pending technology? +
Our multi-angle grading system—using four specific views to assess condition—is patent pending. This approach catches defects that single-photo tools miss, giving you better estimates and more confidence in your grading decisions.
