## FILING STEPS - USPTO WEBSITE

### Step 1: Log Into USPTO
1. Go to: https://www.uspto.gov/patents/apply
2. Click "File online with EFS-Web"
3. Log in with your USPTO account credentials
4. Select "Unregistered eFiler" (unless you're a registered patent attorney)

### Step 2: Start New Application
1. Click "Create New Filing"
2. Select application type: **"Provisional Patent Application"**
3. Click "Continue"

### Step 3: Fill Out Application Data Sheet (ADS)
This is your cover sheet with applicant information.

**Applicant Information:**
- Enter your full legal name
- Enter mailing address
- Enter email and phone
- Citizenship: United States (or your country)
- Entity status: Micro/Small/Large (choose one)

**Correspondence Information:**
- Same as applicant address
- Email where USPTO will send notifications

**Title of Invention:**
```
System and Method for Identifying and Recovering Stolen Collectibles Using Photographic Defect Pattern Analysis
```

**Docket Number:** (Optional - you can enter "COMIC-FP-001" or leave blank)

Click "Continue"

### Step 4: Upload Patent Document
1. Click "Add Document"
2. Select document type: **"Specification"**
3. Upload file: `provisional_patent_comic_fingerprinting.docx`
4. Verify file uploaded successfully
5. Click "Continue"

### Step 5: Review Fee Calculation
USPTO will calculate your fee based on entity status:
- Micro Entity: $130
- Small Entity: $260
- Large Entity: $520

Verify the amount is correct.

### Step 6: Payment
1. Enter credit card information OR use deposit account
2. Confirm payment amount
3. Click "Submit Payment"

### Step 7: Review and Validate
1. USPTO will validate your submission
2. Check for any error messages
3. Fix any issues if flagged
4. Click "Submit Application"

### Step 8: Confirmation
1. You'll receive a confirmation number immediately
2. Screenshot or write down: **Application Number** and **Confirmation Number**
3. Within 1-3 business days: USPTO sends official filing receipt via email
4. Filing receipt includes: **Serial Number** (this is your official patent number)

## IMPORTANT LEGAL NOTES

### Priority Date
Your **filing date** is your priority date. Anyone who files after you cannot claim your invention, even if they independently invented it.

### Confidentiality
USPTO keeps provisional patents **confidential**. They are never published. Only you and USPTO know it exists.

### 12-Month Deadline
You **MUST** file non-provisional within 12 months OR your provisional expires. Set a calendar reminder for **Month 10** to start the process.

### International Protection
To get international patents:
- File PCT (Patent Cooperation Treaty) application within 12 months
- Costs $4k-$10k but covers 150+ countries
- Decide based on market success

### Public Disclosure
You can publicly launch your product now! The provisional filing protects you. But:
- US: 1-year grace period (you're safe)
- International: File PCT within 12 months for foreign protection

## QUESTIONS & TROUBLESHOOTING

### Q: Do I need a patent attorney?
**A:** Not for provisional filing. The document I created follows USPTO standards. For the full patent (Month 10-12), hiring an attorney is strongly recommended.

### Q: What if I make a mistake in the application?
**A:** You cannot amend a provisional after filing. BUT you can file a second provisional with corrections (costs another $130). The earlier filing date is what matters.

### Q: Can I file under Slab Worthy LLC?
**A:** Yes, if Slab Worthy LLC is established. Enter the company name as applicant. If filing as individual, you can assign rights to the company later.

### Q: What if someone steals my idea after I file?
**A:** Your filing date is your priority. Even if they file a patent, yours came first. You win. But don't share the serial number publicly.

### Q: Do I need drawings/diagrams?
**A:** Recommended but not required for provisional. The document I created includes descriptions of drawings. For full patent, professional drawings are required.

### Q: Can I add features later?
**A:** YES! That's the point of provisional. During your 12-month window, note any improvements. Include them in the full patent filing.

### Q: What if I don't file the full patent?
**A:** The provisional expires after 12 months. You lose priority. BUT you can file a new provisional for an updated version if needed.
