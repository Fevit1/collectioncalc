### What We Do

CollectionCalc helps comic book collectors:
1. **Estimate fair market value** of raw (ungraded) comics
2. **Assess condition** using multi-photo AI analysis
3. **Decide whether to submit for professional grading** (CGC/CBCS)
4. **Track and manage** their collection

### What We Are NOT

- NOT a grading company (CGC and CBCS grade comics; we help you decide IF you should use them)
- NOT a marketplace (we don't buy or sell comics)
- NOT a game or entertainment app (we're a utility tool)
- NOT a gambling or speculation platform

### Personality DO's

- Use data to support recommendations

## Tone by Context

### Informational (explaining features, FAQs)
- Clear, educational, patient
- Example: "Slab Worthy? analyzes up to 4 photos of your comic to estimate its grade and help you decide if professional grading makes financial sense."

### Transactional (buttons, CTAs, confirmations)
- Direct, action-oriented, confident
- Example: "Check Another" / "Save to Collection" / "Get Valuation"

### Celebratory (positive results)

## Terminology

### Terms We Use

| Term | Definition | Context |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Raw** | Ungraded comic (not in a CGC/CBCS slab) | "Raw value: $45" |
| **Slabbed** | Professionally graded and encapsulated | "Slabbed value: $85" |
| **Slab** | The plastic case from CGC/CBCS | "Is it worth the slab?" |
| **Assessment** | Our AI-based condition evaluation | "Our assessment suggests VF" |
| **Estimate** | Our calculated market value | "Estimated value: $50-65" |
| **Fair Market Value (FMV)** | Realistic sale price based on recent sales | Used in valuations |
| **Confidence** | How certain we are in our estimate | "High confidence (87%)" |
| **Check** | Using Slab Worthy? to evaluate a comic | "Check another comic" |

### Terms We Avoid

| Avoid | Use Instead | Reason |
|-------|-------------|--------|
| "Grade" (as verb) | "Assess" or "Check" | CGC/CBCS grade; we assess |
| "Worth" | "Value" or "Estimated value" | "Worth" implies certainty |
| "Price" | "Value" or "Estimate" | We don't set prices |
| "Accurate" | "Confident" | We can't guarantee accuracy |
| "Guarantee" | "Estimate" or "Suggest" | No guarantees on values |
| "Win/Lose" | "Benefit/Cost" | Not gambling language |
| "Score" | "Grade" or "Result" | Not a game |

### Comic Grading Scale Reference

When discussing grades, use standard terminology:

| Grade | Abbreviation | Numeric | Description |
|-------|--------------|---------|-------------|
| Mint | MT | 10.0 | Perfect |
| Near Mint | NM | 9.4 | Nearly perfect |
| Very Fine | VF | 8.0 | Minor wear |
| Fine | FN | 6.0 | Moderate wear |
| Very Good | VG | 4.0 | Significant wear |
| Good | G | 2.0 | Heavy wear |
| Fair | FR | 1.5 | Major wear |
| Poor | PR | 1.0 | Severe damage |

## Content Guidelines for GenAI

### Prompt Context

When generating content for CollectionCalc, GenAI tools should understand:

```
CollectionCalc is a pre-grading assessment tool for comic book collectors. 
It uses AI to analyze photos of comics and estimate their condition/grade, 
then calculates whether professional grading (CGC/CBCS) makes financial sense.

The brand voice is: expert, approachable, honest, helpful.
The brand is NOT: salesy, hyperbolic, game-like, or gambling-adjacent.

Key product: "Slab Worthy?" - a 4-photo grading assessment flow (Patent Pending)
Key output: "Slab Report" - the assessment results with grade estimate and recommendation
```

### Content Boundaries

**GenAI MAY generate:**
- Educational content about comic grading
- Feature explanations and how-to guides
- FAQ answers
- Error messages and help text
- Marketing copy that follows brand voice
- Social media posts (informative, helpful)

**GenAI should AVOID generating:**
- Price guarantees or specific value predictions
- Legal or financial advice
- Gambling or "winning/losing" language
- Competitive attacks on CGC/CBCS
- Claims about accuracy or guarantees
- Overly promotional or hyperbolic content

### Example Prompts for GenAI

**Good prompt:**
> "Write a short description of the Slab Worthy? feature for the CollectionCalc homepage. Voice: expert, approachable, honest. Avoid hype or gambling language."

**Good prompt:**
> "Generate 3 FAQ answers explaining why CollectionCalc shows confidence percentages instead of exact values. Brand voice: transparent, helpful, professional."
